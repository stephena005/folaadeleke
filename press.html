Long Bio — 180 words

Fola Adeleke® is a London-based fine art print house working at the intersection of contemporary portraiture, typographic restraint, and editorial design. Founded in 2026, the studio operates on a single, unusual principle: every piece is a limited edition, released on Sunday at 12PM BST, and never restocked.

The work draws from the visual language of the broadsheet, the fashion editorial, and the archive. Prints are produced on museum-grade archival paper at A1 and A2, and priced deliberately to sit between the accessible and the collectible — fine art for the first wall of an adult home.

The inaugural collection Sisterhood, Loud Celebration, and No Voice Chorus marks the studio's opening position: a meditation on femininity as chorus, celebration, and the long work of being heard. Subsequent drops expand the archive weekly, with subscribers to the studio's newsletter — Fola Adeleke’s Notes — receiving first access, process notes, and occasional pre-release previews.

Studio Statement

We believe a print should hold its own in a room. Not shout. Not apologise. We print small, we print once, and we don't restock — because a limit is what turns a picture into a record of a moment. Sundays at 12PM are our release window because ritual is a kind of promise, and we keep it.

The work is concerned with voice: who gets one, how it carries, and what it costs to raise it. Every drop is a sentence in a longer argument, which is why the archive matters more than any single piece. We make for the collector who is building a room, not decorating one.

Press Release

For Immediate Release

LONDON, 2026 — Fola Adeleke® launches today as a limited edition fine art print house, releasing its first three works — Sisterhood, Loud Celebration, and No Voice Chorus — on folaadeleke.com. Each piece is available in A1 and A2, printed on museum-grade archival paper, and priced from £95.

The studio introduces a disciplined release model to the fine art print market: one drop per week, every Sunday at 12PM BST, with no restocks. Once an edition closes, it is closed. The format borrows from luxury fashion's drop culture and re-tunes it for the archive — rewarding subscribers, collectors, and curators who move decisively.

The inaugural collection explores femininity as a chorus: three portraits in conversation about solidarity, visibility, and the long work of being heard. Sisterhood sits at the centre; Loud Celebration is its joy; No Voice Chorus is its unfinished business.

Alongside the shop, the studio launches Fola Adeleke’s Notes, a subscriber letter containing process documentation, archive reissues, and pre-release access. A press programme is in place for galleries, curators, and editors; high-resolution assets and interview requests are available on application.
